## Scope, corpus, and synthesis method

This Phase 3 artifact integrates the frozen 48-source corpus—30 peer-reviewed
sources, 12 official/professional primary sources, and six first-party product
sources—plus the separately registered supplemental S01. Identity and source
quality were verified in `PHASE_2_SOURCE_VERIFICATION.md`; A16 is treated as
Level V/Grade B, vendor efficacy and privacy-performance claims remain Grade D,
and S01 is not counted as a forty-ninth frozen-corpus source. Repository claims
use finding IDs or precise file/line evidence; external citations use only the
allowed A01–A30, G01–G12, P01–P06, and S01 identifiers.

The synthesis used thematic, framework, and critical-interpretive integration.
It first mapped every source to six themes, then tested convergence, conflict,
silence, rival explanations, and the three futures. Evidence-strength labels
mean: **Strong** = multiple independent, fit-for-claim sources plus compatible
repository evidence; **Moderate** = convergent but indirect, small, or
context-limited evidence; **Emerging** = one or two relevant sources or a design
hypothesis; **Gap** = the decision-relevant observation has not been made.

## Integrated thematic synthesis

### Theme 1 — The project has strong computation evidence and missing judgment evidence

**Evidence strength: Strong.** The repository demonstrates a broad keyless
software gate: type checking and build pass; 9,507 tests pass; route/resource
tests are strong; quick Doctor scoring and hashing are reproducible under named
input/configuration/build conditions (`BASELINE_VERIFICATION.md`; truth registry
S-01, S-02, T-23). It also demonstrates the opposite side of the distinction:
72 real-corpus cases do not run without a private environment variable, the
composite synthetic margin is about 2.9 against a 5.0 gate, the 20-sample
reference is synthetic, and the internal rule channel is recorded at AUC about
0.076 while scene scarcity is about 0.938 (S-04–S-07, S-14;
`server/nvm/analyze/doctor.ts:1655-1669`).

Independent evidence makes the missing warrant explicit. Generated-story
benchmarks find unsettled, weak, or perturbation-fragile automatic evaluation,
while even improved LLM system-level correlation does not establish reliable
instance-level explanation or screenplay diagnosis: Chhun et al. (2022)
<!--ref:A10--><!--anchor:page:5794-5836-->, Chhun et al. (2024)
<!--ref:A11--><!--anchor:page:1122-1142-->, and Guan et al. (2021)
<!--ref:A12--><!--anchor:page:6394-6407-->. Measurement theory separately
requires evidence for the exact construct, interpretation, use, and consequence,
not merely repeatability: Flake and Fried (2020)
<!--ref:A17--><!--anchor:page:456-465-->, Messick (1995)
<!--ref:A18--><!--anchor:page:741-749-->, and AERA, APA, and NCME (2014)
<!--ref:G01--><!--anchor:quote:Standards%20for%20educational%20and%20psychological%20testing-->.

**Synthesis.** The receipt substrate can authenticate *what computation was
performed*; it cannot authenticate *that the score meaning is correct*. That is
why the same reproducibility feature is an asset under an evidence-first Clinic
and a possible certainty amplifier under an unvalidated Coverage Authority.
No writer demand and no score validity have been established.

### Theme 2 — Writer-centered assistance is contextual and role-plural, but its value remains unobserved

**Evidence strength: Moderate for design direction; Gap for demand and
outcomes.** The most population-aligned frozen study reports differentiated
screenwriter workflow stages and desired AI roles among 23 interviewees, while
adjacent work emphasizes relationships, expressive intent, reader experience,
and selective writer control: Tang et al. (2025)
<!--ref:A02--><!--anchor:page:1-18-->, Cheng and Frens (2022)
<!--ref:A01--><!--anchor:page:1-29-->, Kreminski and Martens (2022)
<!--ref:A04--><!--anchor:page:74-82-->, Yuan et al. (2022)
<!--ref:A06--><!--anchor:page:841-852-->, and Lee et al. (2022)
<!--ref:A07--><!--anchor:page:1-19-->. Mirowski et al. (2023)
<!--ref:A03--><!--anchor:page:1-34--> adds formative professional reflections on
co-writing control and failure, not coverage validation.

The negative control is essential: favorable experience with machine
suggestions did not necessarily improve the tested writing artifacts in Clark
et al. (2018) <!--ref:A05--><!--anchor:page:329-340-->. Supplemental S01 is
unusually relevant but remains narrow: in a 14-participant short-term study,
participants rated DuoDrama above specific AI-generated comparator conditions
on some self-reported feedback and reflection dimensions; it did not compare
against professional human coverage or independently score revised scripts
(Tang et al., 2026, DOI `10.1145/3772318.3790568`)
<!--ref:S01--><!--anchor:page:1-20-->.

**Synthesis.** These sources support a falsifiable interface hypothesis:
capture the writer's current revision intent, expose bounded perspectives, and
let the writer choose and verify an action. They do not establish that writers
want this product, will upload a real draft, will revise better, will return, or
will pay. The implemented Doctor currently cannot receive the full writer task,
form, completeness, or desired note type (truth registry S-09; perspective
findings A1 and E2), so context is not merely missing from presentation—it is
missing from the flagship analytic contract.

### Theme 3 — Human judgment adds context only if plurality and measurement discipline remain visible

**Evidence strength: Strong for method; Moderate for screenplay transfer.** A
future reader panel cannot be treated as a simple truth oracle. Rating
aggregation can reverse inferred preferences, both human and LLM judges show
tested biases, and evaluation design determines what agreement means:
Ethayarajh and Jurafsky (2022)
<!--ref:A13--><!--anchor:page:6056-6070-->, Chen et al. (2024)
<!--ref:A15--><!--anchor:page:8301-8327-->, van der Lee et al. (2019)
<!--ref:A14--><!--anchor:page:355-368-->, Hallgren (2012)
<!--ref:A19--><!--anchor:page:23-34-->, Artstein and Poesio (2008)
<!--ref:A20--><!--anchor:page:555-596-->, and Koo and Li (2016)
<!--ref:A21--><!--anchor:page:155-163-->. Creative-product studies indicate that
expertise and domain match can improve consistency, but agreement remains
different from validity: Kaufman et al. (2008)
<!--ref:A22--><!--anchor:page:171-178--> and Kaufman et al. (2009)
<!--ref:A23--><!--anchor:page:223-233-->.

**Synthesis.** Future B has the highest ceiling for asking what the writer
meant, interpreting ambiguity, and owning a challenge. It also creates new
failure channels—reader variance, anchoring by the machine scaffold,
confidential access, supply, turnaround, appeals, and contribution margin
(failure modes FM-12, FM-20). The right benchmark target is therefore not one
averaged “professional truth.” It is a predeclared set of constructs with
individual reader distributions, rationales, appropriate agreement statistics,
adjudication rules, and uncertainty (truth registry S-11–S-13).

### Theme 4 — Trust must be calibrated through contestability, not maximized through polish

**Evidence strength: Strong cross-domain; direct writer evidence missing.** The
foundational and systematic-review evidence distinguishes trust from reliance
and argues that reliance should track actual capability: Lee and See (2004)
<!--ref:A24--><!--anchor:page:50-80--> and Mehrotra et al. (2024)
<!--ref:A25--><!--anchor:page:1-45-->. Controlled studies show that reducing
overreliance can lower preference and that verification depends on task and
explanation cost: Buçinca et al. (2021)
<!--ref:A27--><!--anchor:page:1-21--> and Vasconcelos et al. (2023)
<!--ref:A28--><!--anchor:page:1-38-->. Visible errors can also induce underuse,
not only overuse: Dietvorst et al. (2015)
<!--ref:A29--><!--anchor:page:114-126-->.

Human–AI composition is similarly conditional. Across more than 100
experiments, combinations augmented humans on average but did not show average
synergy over the stronger member; capability and information asymmetry must be
designed and tested rather than assumed: Vaccaro et al. (2024)
<!--ref:A26--><!--anchor:page:2293-2303--> and Hemmer et al. (2025)
<!--ref:A30--><!--anchor:page:979-1002-->.

**Synthesis.** The design objective is neither “make writers trust the score”
nor “add a human.” It is to make evidence inexpensive to inspect, make
uncertainty and lineage visible, elicit an independent writer view where
appropriate, make challenge/reversal cheap, and escalate high-impact ambiguity
to an accountable person. Receipts should prove computation and revision
history without rhetorically proving craft truth (FM-01, FM-13). Whether this
interaction produces appropriate reliance in screenwriters is a direct P0/P1
research question, not an imported result.

### Theme 5 — Trustworthiness is a socio-technical gate, not a scoring property

**Evidence strength: Strong repository evidence plus authoritative risk
identification.** Current critical findings are concentrated at boundaries:
same-build export/import is incompatible; malformed import can destroy good
state; “full” export is partial; generative routes use the wrong limiter; no
`.dockerignore` protects ignored drafts/secrets from the build context; raw
provider-test errors are logged; and there is no complete writer-facing draft
lifecycle contract (PX-07A/B; QO-01–QO-04; ST-01–ST-06; truth registry T-01–T-22).
The failure register shows how these defects interact rather than merely add:
FM-03 × FM-11 makes the recovery promise both destructive and incomplete;
FM-04 × FM-07 places an unlisted build/cache processor inside the confidentiality
boundary; FM-09 × FM-15 can make a changed deep result unreconstructable.

External guidance supports the risk categories without proving violation or
compliance. NIST frames lifecycle GAI risk management (NIST, 2024)
<!--ref:G02--><!--anchor:quote:Artificial%20intelligence%20risk%20management%20framework%3A%20Generative%20artificial%20intelligence%20profile-->, FTC guidance
identifies substantiation and confidential-data commitments as material claim
areas (FTC, 2023) <!--ref:G07--><!--anchor:quote:Keep%20your%20AI%20claims%20in%20check-->
and (FTC, 2024)
<!--ref:G08--><!--anchor:quote:AI%20companies%3A%20Uphold%20your%20privacy%20and%20confidentiality%20commitments-->,
and WCAG 2.2 supplies testable technical accessibility criteria (W3C, 2024)
<!--ref:G06--><!--anchor:quote:Web%20Content%20Accessibility%20Guidelines%20%28WCAG%29%202.2-->.

**Synthesis.** Content recoverability, confidentiality, accessible control,
cost containment, and artifact identity are part of whether feedback is
trustworthy. A better scoring study cannot compensate for destructive restore;
a privacy notice cannot compensate for an unsafe build context; deterministic
receipts cannot compensate for mutable release identity. These are
noncompensable gates for A, B, and C, not secondary quality points. The legal
observations remain risk flags for deployment-specific counsel, not findings
that STORYMACHINE has violated a law (truth registry L-01–L-08).

### Theme 6 — The defensible asset is an evidence relationship, not an output checklist

**Evidence strength: Moderate market observation; Gap for commercial
outcomes.** The Phase 2 benchmark found that rapid reports, long category lists,
scores, Pass/Consider/Recommend, chat, and multi-pass language are already
available across peer, automated, general-purpose, and human alternatives
(`PHASE_2_COMPETITIVE_INDUSTRY.md:396-475`; P01–P06 in the literature matrix).
No reviewed vendor page established an independently replicated held-out
real-screenplay feedback-quality benchmark; that is a dated public-evidence
finding, not evidence that a vendor fails in practice (`PHASE_2_COMPETITIVE_INDUSTRY.md:26-32`;
source-verification disposition for P01–P06).

**Synthesis.** Rule count, pass count, report length, speed, and a fluent model
perspective are visibly common and therefore weak *publicly observable
differentiation* in the reviewed set. A potentially harder-to-copy stack would
combine rights-clean real drafts, multiple-reader disagreement, writer intent,
versioned draft diffs, accepted/rejected/challenged findings, blinded revision
outcomes, receipt provenance, and demonstrable deletion/confidentiality
operations (`PHASE_2_COMPETITIVE_INDUSTRY.md:515-530`; truth registry P-08–P-13,
L-08). This is a hypothesis about future defensibility that still requires
rights, use, retention, imitation-cost, and commercial evidence—not a currently
owned dataset, network, demand signal, or moat.

### Theme 7 — The strategic result is conditional, not universal

**Evidence strength: Decision-analytic, not empirical utility.** The frozen
three-future model gives C (Reflective Draft Clinic) 83.0, B (Reader Studio)
71.4, and A (Coverage Authority) 48.8 under the evidence-first base weights. It
also gives B the lead under a premium-nuance/learning world, A a narrow lead
under a scale-and-moat world that presupposes a strong P1 prior, and C the lead
under catastrophic trust-error minimization (`PHASE_3_COMPETING_FUTURES.md`
§§5–7). These are ordinal judgments and value sensitivity, not probabilities,
market forecasts, or measured user utility.

**Synthesis.** The numeric model is a transparent owner-prior exercise with
correlated criteria and mixed current/future ratings, not independent evidence
that writers prefer C. Its robust current conclusion is a sequence: preserve a
stop/hold control, repair or disable critical trust boundaries, run the bounded
P0 stimulus, and branch only on observed evidence. C is a reversible concept to
test *if* writers reject scalar authority but show pull for located revision
evidence. B remains a conditional branch if writers require accountable
contextual interpretation and the fully loaded service model works. A remains
a real option only after P0 and real P1 validation. No future is currently
launch eligible; P0 is unmet and all share failed/not-demonstrated noncompensable gates
(`PHASE_3_COMPETING_FUTURES.md` §4; truth registry F-02/F-04, S-04, T-01/T-02,
T-16/T-20).

## Rival explanations and stress tests

1. **The engine may be capable; the current corpus may simply be inadequate.**
   This rival explanation is plausible. The 20-sample synthetic reference and
   absent ordinary-CI real corpus cannot estimate the engine's upper bound.
   However, it strengthens—not weakens—the requirement to withhold authority
   until a rights-clean held-out study tests the claim (S-04–S-07, S-13–S-14).

2. **Scene scarcity may intentionally measure draft completeness rather than
   craft quality.** That interpretation could be defensible for one declared
   whole-feature readiness use. The current route does not know form,
   completeness, target length, or revision intent, so the same term becomes
   construct-irrelevant variance for shorts, excerpts, or partials (S-09–S-10;
   FM-02). The falsifier is a form-conditioned, length-matched evaluation.

3. **The synthesis may prefer C because its evidence philosophy rewards
   humility, not because writers do.** Correct: C's ranking is partly a value
   judgment about claim honesty, agency, and reversibility. Sensitivity analysis
   makes this explicit—B wins when nuance/learning dominate and A wins only when
   scale/moat dominate under a strong P1 prior. P0 must test whether writers see
   evidence-first assistance as useful or evasive.

4. **OASIS breadth may serve a different valuable segment.** The current audit
   asks about the pre-reader screenwriter wedge, so default OASIS exposure is a
   confound for that test. It may retain Labs option value; no evidence here
   establishes that the distinct simulation job lacks demand (truth registry
   F-05/F-06, P-06/P-07; FM-23).

5. **The restore defects may be isolated route bugs rather than an architectural
   flaw.** The stale version constant and destructive ordering are bounded
   defects, but incomplete export, title-page loss, in-memory Yjs, multiple
   screenplay authorities, and raw reset copy show a broader undefined recovery
   aggregate (T-01–T-06, T-10; FM-03/FM-11). The strongest explanation is both:
   immediate bugs sit inside an unresolved authority model.

6. **A human service may validate the human, not the software.** This is a live
   threat to Future B. A pilot must compare independent-human, machine-only, and
   assisted conditions, blind some readers to machine scores, preserve override
   reasons, and maintain a fully loaded cost ledger (FM-12, FM-13, FM-20).

7. **Public competitor evidence may understate private validation or operating
   strength.** Correct. P01–P06 and wider vendor pages were admitted only as
   offering/claim observables. The audit's competitive conclusion is limited to
   public differentiation and evidence, not internal performance.

8. **Repairing all engineering gaps before learning could itself delay P0.**
   The protocol does not require production hardening before any conversation.
   It requires immediate repair of critical defects that can destroy/expose
   drafts or create unbounded spend, plus a bounded certified P0 posture. Lower
   priority release, scale, and performance work remains sequenced by actual
   exposure and writer evidence (F-04; `PHASE_3_FAILURE_MODE_REGISTER.md` §7).

## Theoretical integration

### Toulmin validity chain

Future A's present claim can be decomposed as follows: the **claim** is that a
headline score/verdict expresses professional screenplay readiness; the
**data** are rule findings, scene signals, synthetic calibration, and
deterministic recomputation; the missing **warrant** is that those signals and
their formula support that exact interpretation and consequence on real drafts;
the missing **backing** is a rights-clean held-out multi-reader study; the
necessary **qualifiers** are form, completeness, intent, uncertainty, domain,
mode, build, and abstention; and the **rebuttals** include disagreement, metric
gaming, parser/semantic failure, and subgroup/genre error (S-04–S-17; FM-01,
FM-02, FM-05, FM-06).

Future C changes the claim rather than pretending to fill the warrant: a
versioned observation occurred at a named location under a named build and may
be useful to the writer's declared revision decision. That narrower claim is
better supported by present receipts, but its usefulness warrant remains P0
evidence and cannot be supplied by architecture alone (P-01–P-04).

### Four-framework bridge

1. **Measurement validity** asks whether the score meaning and use are
   supported, not merely repeatable.
2. **Human–AI reliance** asks whether people use assistance appropriately, not
   whether they like or trust it.
3. **Socio-technical security and content integrity** asks whether the writer
   can safely control, recover, delete, and attribute confidential work across
   people, providers, builds, and stores.
4. **Real-options strategy** asks which next test creates useful evidence while
   preserving the ability to change course.

Together these frameworks explain why C is the base-case low-regret hypothesis:
it narrows the unsupported measurement claim, supports contestability, reuses
the receipt substrate, and preserves options to add validated measures or human
escalation. They also explain why C is not universally superior: if direct
writer evidence makes contextual human interpretation dominant, B wins; if a
strong P1 validates consequential automation and scale/moat dominate priorities,
A can win (`PHASE_3_COMPETING_FUTURES.md` §§6–8).

## Direct answer to the research question

The most credible route is **not yet a launchable product design or a selected
product future**. It is a gated learning strategy with an explicit stop/hold
