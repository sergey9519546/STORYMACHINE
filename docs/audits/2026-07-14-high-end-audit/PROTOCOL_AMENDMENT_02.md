# Protocol Amendment 02 — Late-Breaking Screenplay-Reflection Study

**Date:** 2026-07-14  
**Trigger:** Current-source verification before Phase 3 synthesis  
**Changes the research question or decision gates:** No  
**Adds evidence after the registered 48-source Phase 2 corpus:** Yes

## Discovery and verification

The exact-title source *DuoDrama: Supporting Screenplay Refinement Through
LLM-Assisted Human Reflection* was discovered while checking current
screenwriter-specific research after the Phase 2 search ledger had been frozen.
The registered search strings did not name DuoDrama, so silently inserting it
into the 48-source screening flow would make that flow inaccurate.

The source is therefore recorded as supplemental evidence, not retroactively
counted in the registered corpus:

- Microsoft Research identifies the published work as CHI 2026 and reports a formative
  study with nine professional screenwriters plus an evaluation with fourteen
  professional screenwriters:
  https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/duodrama-supporting-screenplay-refinement-through-llm-assisted-human-reflection/
- arXiv record 2602.05854 identifies the same title/authors, submission date,
  CHI 2026 acceptance, and sample descriptions:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05854
- The ACM record independently resolves as DOI
  `10.1145/3772318.3790568`; the Phase 2 source-verification report found no
  retraction or integrity signal.

## Evidentiary treatment

**Classification:** Level VI small HCI user study; Grade B; supplemental ID S01.

The study is unusually applicable because it examines screenplay refinement,
professional screenwriters, feedback, and reflection rather than generic text
generation. It supports the plausibility of organizing assistance around
internal character experience and external story perspective.

It does **not** establish market demand, durable revision improvement, scoring
validity, commercial retention, or general superiority. The fourteen
participants compared DuoDrama with specific AI-generated conditions—not
professional human coverage—and the outcomes were short-term self-reports, not
blind screenplay-quality measures. The paper reports a large test family
without a multiple-comparison adjustment. The authors built and evaluated the
system; two authors were affiliated with Microsoft Research Asia, the work
acknowledges a Microsoft-supported collaboration, and the prototype used Azure.
The samples are small, the record is recent, and the favorable result aligns
with one candidate future in this audit. To control confirmation bias, S01 may
strengthen a conclusion only when it converges with the registered corpus and
repository evidence; it may not decide the strategic option by itself.

## Effect on synthesis

The Phase 2 corpus remains 48 independently verified sources. Phase 3 may cite
S01 as a transparently late-breaking, highly relevant but limited source. The
final report must disclose its supplemental status wherever it materially
supports the reflective-feedback design.
